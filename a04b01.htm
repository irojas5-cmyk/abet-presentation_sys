Student Outcomes

Student Outcomes are prominently listed in the “ChemE Major” link on the home page of the departmental website: www.che.uic.edu . Additionally, relevant Student Outcomes are included in all undergraduate course syllabi, and class time is devoted to explaining how the knowledge, skills and principles developed by the course in question contribute to the understanding and abilities represented by the Student Outcomes. The processes for revisiting and updating PEOs also afford opportunities to reconsider Student Outcomes and amend them as necessary. The Student Outcomes from 2014 were revised and coincide directly with the ABET Student Outcomes 1-7. The table below shows the Student Outcomes as related to the ABET Student Outcomes and the Program Educational Objectives.

# Description ABET PEO
1 An ability to identify, formulate, and solve complex engineering problems by applying principles of engineering, science, and mathematics. 1 1
2 An ability to apply engineering design to produce solutions that meet specified needs with consideration of public health, safety, and welfare, as well as global, cultural, social, environmental, and economic factors 2 1, 2, 3
3 An ability to communicate effectively with a range of audiences. 3 3
4 An ability to recognize ethical and professional responsibilities in engineering situations and make informed judgments, which must consider the impact of engineering solutions in global, economic, environmental, and societal contexts. 4 2, 3
5 An ability to function effectively on a team whose members together provide leadership, create a collaborative and inclusive environment, establish goals, plan tasks, and meet objectives. 5 3
6 An ability to develop and conduct appropriate experimentation, analyze and interpret data, and use engineering judgment to draw conclusions. 6 1
7 An ability to acquire and apply new knowledge as needed, using appropriate learning strategies. 7 4
Student Outcomes are updated through a self-study process by the department faculty, considering feedback from students, alumni and employers. Initial ideas are presented to the External Advisory Board before being finalized and formally accepted by faculty vote. The PEOs are then updated on the departmental website, and assessment procedures are adjusted accordingly. More information on the process is available in the Self-Study Report.
