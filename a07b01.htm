Vikas Berry
Professor and Department Head
PhD, Virginia Polytechnic Institute & State University, 2006

226A EIB
Phone: (312) 996-2342
Email: vikasb@uic.edu

Biographical sketch Dr. Berry is Professor and Head of UIC’s Department of Chemical Engineering, where he joined the faculty in 2015. He received his PhD in chemical engineering from Virginia Tech in 2006. He obtained his master’s degree from the University of Kansas in 2003 and his bachelor’s degree from the Indian Institute of Technology-Delhi in 1999. Berry has made pioneering contributions in the fields of graphene quantum materials and bio/nano technologies. He has earned many honors, including the NSF-CAREER Award in 2011, the Sigma Xi Outstanding Junior Scientist Award in 2010, and the Big 12 Fellowship in 2009, and a chaired-professorship appointment (the William H. Honstead Professorship at Kansas State University) from 2012 to 2014. His work on graphene biointerfaces is considered a nodal point in the evolution of graphene’s biological applications. Berry is a member of the editorial board of Scientific Reports (Nature Publishing Group) and the Journal of Nanoscience Letters. Research results from Berry’s group have been published in several high-impact journals, including Nature Photonics, Nature Communications, Nano Letters, Advanced Materials, and Small, and have been featured in Nature, Science News, Washington Post, The Economist, the Wall Street Journal, Discover, Chemical Engineering Progress, and Physics World, among other news outlets. Berry’s research is funded by the NSF, DoD, and industry.

Research interests:
2D Nanomaterials (Graphene,Metal Dichalcogenides, BN), Bio-Nanotechnology, Materials Science, Electronic Materials, Molecular Devices, Sensors

Biographical sketch Michael Amiridis is Professor and Chancellor of the University of Illinois at Chicago, where he joined the university in 2015. Prior to his becoming UIC chancellor, Amiridis spent over two decades as a professor, researcher and senior administrator at the University of South Carolina. He received his PhD in Chemical Engineering at the University of Wisconsin, Madison, in 1991. As UIC’s chancellor, Amiridis heads Chicago’s largest university campus, with more than 33,300 students. The university, which holds the distinguished R1 research status in the Carnegie Classification of Institutions of Higher Education, ranks among the top 65 out of more than 650 national universities in federal research funding. He has earned many honors, including the NSF-CAREER Award in 1996, Top Cited Article in Catalyst Today in 2004, and a Research Achievement Award from the University of South Carolina in 2005.

Research interests:
Heterogeneous catalysis and chemical kinetics, Synthetic strategies, Catalytic destruction of chlorinated aromatic compounds over supported transition metal oxides, Heterogeneous synthesis of flavanones

Betul Bilgin
Clinical Assistant Professor
PhD, Michigan State University

144 EIB
Phone: (312) 413-3940
Email: bbilgin@uic.edu

Biographical sketch Dr. Betul Bilgin has been teaching the Senior Design I and II courses for 6 years and Introduction to Thermodynamics for two years. Since her appointment in 2014 she has been exploring active learning, peer instruction, team-based, hands-on, application-based techniques in her classes to fully engage her students. Dr. Bilgin has improved the Senior Design course by integrating ethical, economical, safety, environmental and societal aspects of chemical engineering into her course and introducing structured requirements. As a result of her efforts, she was selected as a UIC Teaching Scholar for Spring 2017, named as an American Institute of Chemical Engineers (AIChE) “35 under 35” winner in the education category for 2017 and named as American Society for Engineering Education (ASEE) “20 under 40” awardee for 2018. Since her arrival in 2014, Dr. Bilgin has helped to create a community within the department to bridge the gap between the industry and academia. Hence, she has been involved in both the Student Chapter, the Chicago Local sections of AIChE, AIChE Education Division and ASEE. She was able to increase the number of industrial mentors from six to fifteen since her arrival. This led to a very effective communication between the industry and department, which soon led to other collaborations. She attended the ASEE Chemical Engineering Summer School in July 2017 and the National Effective Teaching Institute (NETI) Workshop in May 2017.

Research interests:
Engineering Education
Michael Caracotsios
Clinical Associate Professor
PhD, University of Wisconsin, Madison

President/Founder, AthenaVisual, Inc., Naperville, IL

244 EIB
Phone: (312) 413-3777
Email: mcaracot@uic.edu

Biographical sketch Dr. Michael Caracotsios retired from UOP/Honeywell in 2015 and teaches and develops academic material and computational tools for courses in Thermodynamics (CHE 301), Process Simulation and Design (CHE 433) and Process Control (CHE 341). He is also the President and Founder of AthenaVisual, Inc., a company that promotes the use of Athena Visual Studio, a state-of-the-art engineering software for mathematical modeling and data analysis. Michael has supervised a number of undergraduate research programs that have resulted in state-of-the-art computational software for Thermodynamics, Process Control and Heat Exchanger design. Most recently he supervised an undergraduate research project that resulted in the development of an Excel based tool used for cost estimation of chemical plants as well as financial calculations. All computational tools are seamlessly integrated into the lectures that allow the students to solve complex real-life program that Michael brings into the classroom from his 30 years of industrial experience. Dr. Caracotsios graduated from the University of Wisconsin-Madison with a Ph.D. in Chemical Engineering. Michael is the author of two patents, twelve publications, and numerous presentations at National and International conferences. He is also co-author of the book “Computer-Aided Design of Chemically Reactive Systems” published by John Wiley and Sons, Inc. in 2006. Michael is a strong supporter of the idea to give students free textbooks. Towards this goal he has been writing and distributing for free lecture notes on the subjects of Thermodynamics, Simulation and Process Control. Michael frequently invites experts from the industry to give seminars on a variety of subjects ranging from process design, and control to safety and leadership themes. He also visits local high schools and promotes Chemical Engineering to young students stressing the importance of the profession to the welfare of the society. He participates in the Freshman Engineering Success Program (FESP) as well as all undergraduate student activities in the department. Michael is an active member of the American Institute of Chemical Engineers.

Research interests:
Multiphase Chemical Reactor Modeling, Catalyst Deactivation and Life Prediction, Bayesian Inference in Statistical Analysis, Nonlinear Parameter Estimation in Chemical and Biological Systems, Model Discrimination and Optimal Experimental Design.
