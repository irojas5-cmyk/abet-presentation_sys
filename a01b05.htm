Continuous Improvement Actions based on SO Assessment

Student outcomes evaluated by the direct assessment method provide input for developing recommendations and actions for continuous improvement. From the periodic course rubric assessments, the instructors analyze the average scores and the number of students attaining a satisfactory, developing or unsatisfactory ratings. Based on these results, recommendations for continuous improvements are made and incorporated into the courses. Samples of course rubric assessment, results, and continuous improvement actions are given below. The Senior Exit survey (assessment method #4) is also used as an indirect method to determine how well the students are perceiving that they are attaining the SOs. The ratings from the senior exit survey correlate well with the direct assessment methods.

The Engineering Expo judging rubrics are also used to assess the attainment of the SO’s by the students on a yearly basis. Based on the analysis from the rubrics on the capstone projects in Spring 2020, the CHE 397 senior design instructor, Professor Bilgin, recommended continuous improvement actions that will be incorporated into CHE 397 senior design course to improve the capstone project team's project.

Based on our Level 3 and Level 4 analysis of the attainment by different assessment methods and attainment for all SO’s, the department initiated a ‘Curriculum Day’ to discuss which aspects of the curriculum need of improvement and to recommend action items. The Curriculum Day was held in Fall 2018 with a majority of faculty present. The meeting consisted of faculty discussing the courses they teach and how to improve the content and student attainment of the SOs, with feedback from the rest of the faculty. In addition, there were 5 topic-specific lectures; ‘Best Practices in Teaching’ by Professor Wedgewood, ‘Best Practices in mentoring UGs for research’ by Professor Sharma, ‘Teaching Methods’ by Professor Bilgin, a lecture on one of our extracurricular student learning experiences, ‘Lessons from the Chem-E-Car Experience’, by Professor Singh and a Discussion on Improving student experience, led by Professors Berry and Nitsche. The meeting resulted in several action items to improve the curriculum. The Curriculum Day agenda and minutes with action items are given below. The department plans on holding a Curriculum Day every few years to improve our program based on our Level 3/Level 4 SO assessment.

    • The PEOs are reviewed, updated if necessary, and approved each year at a faculty meeting. The most recent faculty meeting where the PEOs were reviewed took place on August 3, 2020; no changes were deemed necessary. Also, faculty attending the EAB meetings participate in the discussion, review, and approval of the PEOs together with our External Advisory Board members. The most recent EAB meeting took place on August 14, 2020. The PEOs were approved without any changes. In addition to the review of the PEOs by our industrial constituents at the EAB meetings, a PEO review survey is administered to industrial constituents attending the annual Engineering EXPO and annual Fall ChE Industry Day. The PEOs are also reviewed using the same survey by our alumni attending the annual Engineering EXPO and annual ChE Industry Day. A more comprehensive annual online alumni survey is also sent out to our alumni. The 2020 survey resulted in over 40 alumni reviewing and providing feedback of how the PEOs have helped them in their careers. All PEOs received an average score of greater than 4 using a scale of 1 (lowest) to 5 (highest), which we concluded as approval of the PEOs by our alumni.

    Periodically, the PEOs are also reviewed at faculty activities such as Faculty Retreats and in combination with discussions of our Student Learning Outcomes at course curriculum improvement activities such as the Curriculum Day meeting held in 2018. The PEOs are also discussed when the Student Learning Outcomes are periodically reviewed individually by instructors in relation to the courses that they teach. The last review of the PEOs and SLOs occurred during the summer of 2019.

    • Our students are critically important stakeholders in the curriculum and policies of the Chemical Engineering department, and we consult them regularly. However, students are not in as strong a position to determine the appropriate aspects and levels of professional accomplishment until several years after graduation. Their feedback is an integral part of how the department formulates policy, but we do not regard students formally as “constituents” in the review and updating of PEOs. In order that students understand the broader professional context toward which they are being educated, the student learning objectives (SLOs) are reviewed in the undergraduate courses and class discussion is devoted to putting the performance indicators of the course in the professional and career context of the SLOs, and ultimately the PEOs. With this motivation, the PEOs guide the professional development of our students and set targets for their knowledge, skills and principles. In addition, a senior exit survey is given each year to the senior students. This allow the seniors during their final semester of study to self-evaluate the attainment of the SLOs, and ultimately the PEOs.

    • Clarification of Chemistry Courses in Transcripts

    General Chemistry sequence (CHEM 122/123, 124/125) and equivalence of CHEM 112, 114, 116 and 118:

    1) The Chemistry department changed the General Chemistry I and II lecture/lab courses (CHEM 112 and CHEM 114) to CHEM 122/123 and CHEM 124/125 in 2016 to separate the lecture and lab parts of each class. The courses are equivalent and have the same amount of total credit hours (5 credit hours) as the older courses. The only change made was to split the total hours between the lecture (4 cr hr) and lab (1 cr hr).

    2) CHEM 116 and CHEM 118 are the Honors and Chemistry Majors General and Analytical Chemistry courses I and II. The chemical engineering students that are in the Honors College take CHEM 116 and CHEM 118 instead of CHEM 122/123, CHEM 124/125. The courses are equivalent and are shown in our BS Degree NonEngineering and General Education requirements on our website.
