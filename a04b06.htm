Level 1 Analysis:
Reduction of data from assessment Rubrics

For each of the three direct assessment methods (Methods #1, #2, #3 above), individual students are assessed using Performance Indicators on rubric items such as exams questions, lab reports, oral presentations, individual and team projects (including computer coding, process simulation and process design), technical papers, safety quizzes, safety tutorials, and ethics assignments. Performance Indicators are given a “3’ for Satisfactory, “2” for Developing, and “1” for Unsatisfactory. Each PI rubric score is then averaged over all the students, and a total PI average for the rubric item is also calculated. The results for individual PI rubric items are then mapped onto the applicable SOs. One rubric item may (but need not) relate to more than one SO, and each SO may have contributions from several rubric items. This procedure produces direct assessment results on attainment of SOs for; (i) students in a particular course in a given semester (Assessment Method #1 – Course Rubrics), and (ii) a graduating senior class (Assessment Method #2 – Senior Written Examination; and Assessment Method #3 – Engineering Expo Judging rubrics. Examples of each are given below. The complete set of reduction of data from the assessment rubrics for Method #1, #2, and #3 is given in 3. Student Outcomes/Assessment/Direct Assessment.

For the indirect assessment Method #4, a survey was developed to obtain self-assessment of graduating seniors on all the SOs as well as feedback for improvement of the curriculum. The system automatically compiles a summary report that (i) calculates averages and (Satisfactory, Developing, Unsatisfactory) vectors for the Student Outcomes, and (ii) aggregates student responses for each of the feedback questions. The complete set of reduction of data from this assessment is given in 3. Student Outcomes/Assessment/Indirect Assessment. An example of the aggregate student responses for Fall 2016 is given below.

Level 3 Analysis:
Average and satisfactory attainment for all Student Outcomes by method

For each method we combine the average attainment rating and the percentage of students attaining between “Satisfactory” and “Developing” for all SOs year by year into a one-page summary report. This facilitates tracking SOs over time and also comparisons between SOs to determine which aspects of the curriculum are most in need of improvement. Level 4 Analysis:
Satisfactory attainment for all Student Outcomes

For each SO we aggregate the Level 3 results from all three direct assessment methods into an overall percentage of students attaining; 1) Developing or higher, 2) Satisfactory. We also aggregate from all three direct assessment methods the rating scores for each SO. The Level 2 results from indirect assessment (Method #4) are carried over from the Level 3 analysis. The three summaries are shown in Tables 4-5, 4-6 and 4-7 in the SSR, as well as given below, and give us an overall, “bird’s eye” view of SO attainment and which informs which outcomes need improvement.

The direct assessment method summary shown in Tables 4-5, 4-6, and 4-7 indicate that, by and large, the UIC Chemical Engineering undergraduates are sufficiently attaining the departmental Student Outcome criteria of 1) reaching a 65% or better attainment of a Satisfactory score, 2) achieving a 85% or better attainment of a Satisfactory or Developing score, and realizing a rating score above 2.30/3.00. The results from SO #1, SO #2, SO #3, SO #5, and SO #6 are steady and indicate that our students are sufficiently learning the core engineering, mathematics, science, experimentation, teamwork, communication and design skills. Safety/environmental (S &E) and global, economic, societal and ethics (GESE), as covered by SO #4, have shown increasing values from 2015 to 2020 and reflects our efforts to bolster these two student learning outcomes. Student outcome #2 which encompasses the application of engineering design also has high values and reflects the effort to by our industrial mentors to instruct the students in chemical engineering industry design practice with an emphasis on following industry standards in their senior design projects.

  • Results for all Student Outcomes from direct assessment (SSR Tables 4-5, 4-6, and 4-7)

The indirect assessment method summary shown in Table 4-8 presents the average scores from the Senior Exit Survey, which is a self-evaluation by the students to rate how they feel they have achieved learning the skills as given in the 7 student outcomes. All the ratings are between 1 = Excellent and 2 = Strong and correlate well with the three direct assessment method evaluations summarized above.
